# Diabetes in Cats: Practical Daily Care Guide

## What It Is
Feline diabetes mellitus is a condition where a cat cannot regulate blood sugar effectively, usually because insulin production is low or insulin is not used properly. Without treatment, high blood sugar can cause weight loss, dehydration, weakness, and long-term organ stress. The goal of daily care is steady blood sugar control through consistent insulin, meals, and monitoring.

## Common Symptoms to Watch For
- Drinking much more water than usual  
- Larger or more frequent urination  
- Increased appetite with weight loss  
- Low energy, less grooming, or hiding  
- Weakness in the hind legs (walking low on the hocks)  
- Vomiting, poor appetite, or sudden lethargy (urgent signs)

If your cat stops eating, seems very weak, or has vomiting with lethargy, contact your veterinarian immediately.

## Recommended Daily Care Tasks
### Insulin and Feeding Routine
- Give insulin exactly as prescribed, usually every 12 hours at the same times each day.  
- Feed meals at consistent times, typically just before or with insulin doses (for example, around 7 AM and 7 PM).  
- Confirm your cat is eating before giving insulin unless your veterinarian has provided different instructions.  
- Use the same insulin type, syringe, and dose technique every day.

### Monitoring
- Track appetite, water intake, urine output, activity, and body weight in a simple log.  
- Watch for hypoglycemia signs: sudden weakness, tremors, disorientation, or collapse.  
- Keep a glucose source (such as corn syrup) and your emergency vet number accessible.  
- Follow your vet's schedule for glucose curves, fructosamine checks, and dose adjustments.

## Scheduling for Pet Owners
- Plan your day around fixed 12-hour insulin windows; avoid late doses.  
- If work hours vary, arrange a trained backup caregiver for insulin and meals.  
- Keep feeding and medication supplies pre-portioned for weekdays, weekends, and travel.  
- Schedule regular rechecks every few months, or sooner after dose changes.

## Foods and Activities to Avoid
- Avoid high-carbohydrate treats, free-feeding dry food without vet guidance, and sudden diet changes.  
- Avoid skipped meals, double insulin doses, or changing dose times without veterinary advice.  
- Avoid prolonged fasting and unsupervised outdoor activity if your cat is medically unstable.
