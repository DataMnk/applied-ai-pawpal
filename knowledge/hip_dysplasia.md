# Hip Dysplasia in Dogs: Practical Daily Care Guide

## What It Is
Hip dysplasia is a developmental joint problem where the ball-and-socket of the hip does not fit tightly. Over time, this causes joint looseness, inflammation, and arthritis. It is common in large breeds but can affect any dog. Good daily management can reduce pain, protect mobility, and improve quality of life.

## Common Symptoms to Watch For
- Stiffness after rest, especially in the morning  
- Reluctance to jump, climb stairs, or get into the car  
- Bunny-hopping gait when running  
- Limping or swaying in the rear legs  
- Reduced activity, slower walks, or tiring quickly  
- Irritability when hips are touched

If symptoms worsen suddenly, contact your veterinarian promptly.

## Recommended Daily Care Tasks
### Walks and Exercise
- Use **2-3 short, controlled walks** daily (about 10-20 minutes each, depending on your vet's advice).  
- Prefer flat, non-slip surfaces; avoid long hikes and uneven terrain.  
- Add low-impact exercise such as leash walking or veterinarian-approved swimming.  
- Keep activity consistent day to day; avoid "weekend overexertion."

### Exercise Limits
- Stop activity if limping increases, your dog lags, or has trouble rising later.  
- Avoid high-impact play (jumping, sudden turns, chasing balls repeatedly).  
- Use ramps instead of stairs when possible.

### Supplements and Home Support
- Discuss joint supplements with your vet: **omega-3 fatty acids, glucosamine/chondroitin, and green-lipped mussel** are commonly used.  
- Maintain a lean body weight; extra weight significantly worsens hip stress.  
- Use orthopedic bedding and rugs/yoga mats for traction indoors.

## Scheduling for Pet Owners
- **Morning:** brief walk, medications/supplements with food, quick mobility check.  
- **Midday:** short potty walk and gentle movement break.  
- **Evening:** controlled walk, light range-of-motion play, dinner/meds.  
- Plan monthly weight checks and routine vet reassessments every 3-6 months.

## Foods and Activities to Avoid
- Avoid excessive treats, high-calorie table scraps, and rapid weight gain.  
- Avoid slippery floors, roughhousing with larger dogs, repetitive stair running, and jumping off furniture.  
- Do not start pain medications or supplements without veterinary guidance.
