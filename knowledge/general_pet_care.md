# General Daily Pet Care: Practical Guide for Busy Owners

## Why Routine Matters
Pets do best with predictable daily habits. A consistent schedule lowers stress, supports healthy digestion, and makes behavior easier to manage. Even if your day is busy, a simple repeatable routine is more helpful than occasional "perfect" days.

## Feeding on a Consistent Schedule
- Feed at the **same times each day** (most adult pets do well with 2 meals daily).  
- Measure portions to prevent overfeeding and weight gain.  
- Keep treats to about 10% or less of daily calories.  
- If switching food, transition gradually over 5-7 days to reduce stomach upset.

Tip: Use alarms or automatic feeders if your schedule changes often.

## Hydration Basics
- Provide fresh, clean water at all times.  
- Wash water bowls daily to reduce bacteria buildup.  
- Monitor intake; sudden drinking much more or much less than usual can signal illness.  
- During hot weather or after exercise, offer extra water breaks.

## Exercise You Can Actually Maintain
- Aim for **daily movement**, not weekend overexertion.  
- Dogs: regular walks plus short play sessions.  
- Cats: two 10-15 minute interactive play sessions (wand toys, chase games).  
- Match activity level to age, breed, and health status.

A realistic target is better than an intense plan you cannot sustain.

## Mental Enrichment
- Rotate puzzle feeders, sniff games, and chew-safe toys.  
- Practice short training sessions (5 minutes) for focus and bonding.  
- Change toy options every few days to keep interest high.  
- Include calm enrichment too, like licking mats or scent exploration.

## Preventive Care Essentials
- Keep vaccines current based on your veterinarian's schedule.  
- Use year-round flea/tick prevention (and heartworm prevention when recommended).  
- Maintain routine parasite checks and stool testing.  
- Schedule regular wellness exams, even if your pet seems healthy.

## Signs That Need a Vet Visit
Contact your vet if you notice vomiting/diarrhea lasting over 24 hours, not eating, major drinking/urination changes, coughing, limping, breathing difficulty, pain signs, sudden behavior changes, or unexplained weight loss. Seek urgent care immediately for collapse, seizures, severe bleeding, toxin exposure, or trouble breathing.
